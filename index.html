What is Yoga?

Yoga is essentially a spiritual discipline based on an extremely subtle science, which focuses on bringing harmony between mind and body. It is an art and scince of healthy living. The word ‘Yoga’ is derived from the Sanskrit root ‘Yuj’, meaning ‘to join’ or ‘to yoke’ or ‘to unite’. As per Yogic scriptures the practice of Yoga leads to the union of individual consciousness with that of the Universal Consciousness, indicating a perfect harmony between the mind and body, Man & Nature.

ORIGIN

The origins of yoga have been speculated to be most likely developed around the sixth and fifth centuries BCE in ancient India.The chronology of earliest texts describing yoga-practices is unclear, varyingly credited to Hindu Upanishads. The Yoga Sutras of Patanjali date from the first half of the 1st millennium CE, but only gained prominence in the West in the 20th century. Hatha yoga texts emerged around the 11th century with origins in tantra.

Yoga gurus from India later introduced yoga to the west, following the success of Swami Vivekananda in the late 19th and early 20th century. In the 1980s, yoga became popular as a system of physical exercise across the Western world.Yoga in Indian traditions, however, is more than physical exercise; it has a meditative and spiritual core.One of the six major orthodox schools of Hinduism is also called Yoga.

Motivations for yoga practioenrs

Unlike stretching or fitness, yoga is more than just physical postures.Even within the physical practice, yoga is unique because we connect the movement of the body and the fluctuations of the mind to the rhythm of our breath. Connecting the mind, body, and breath helps us to direct our attention inward. Through this process of inward attention, one learns to recognize their habitual thought patterns without labeling them, judging them, or trying to change them. They become more aware of our experiences from moment to moment. The awareness that is cultivated is what makes yoga a practice, rather than a task or a goal to be completed. The body will most likely become much more flexible by doing yoga, and so will your mind. Following chart depicts various reasons why practitioners begin and continue to practice Yoga.

Where do people practice yoga?

Some experts have suggested that having fresh air in a quiet and clean place suits the concentration and awareness that yoga will create. They also suggested not practising yoga in direct sunlight, cold winds or after sun-bathing.

This chart depicts where people in the US are likely to practice Yoga. A large number of people can be seen to practice it at home or at the gym.

When and how often is yoga practiced?

Some experts suggested that the best time for practicing yoga is in the morning before breakfast with the second most conducive time being early evening, around sunset. Others have recommended changing yoga routines according to seasons. One can find the time of the day that best works for them as various people may see different amount of benefits. Yoga should be practiced at least three hours after your latest meal since digestion uses a lot of energy, which will be needed while doing yoga.

Experts also recommended practicing 3 times per week is ideal but a little bit of yoga every day is better than a lot of yoga once or twice per week. The more you practice yoga, the more benefits are experienced. One shouldn’t be constrained by time and can practice a bit allowing for natural expansion of time.
