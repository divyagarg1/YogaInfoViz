What is Yoga?

Yoga is an art and science of healthy living. The word "Yoga' is derived from the Sanskrit root 'Yuj', meaning 'to join' or 'to yoke' or 'to unite'. As per Yogic scriptures the practice of Yoga leads to the union of individual consciousness with that of the Universal Consciousness, indicating a perfect harmony between the mind and body, Man & Nature.

Yoga is a group of physical, mental, and spiritual practices or disciplines. The physical aspect of yoga is comprised of postures, known as asanas. Another element of yoga is breathing exercises, called pranayama. The breath and the mind are very tightly connected, so by working on the breath you can change your mental states, and vice-versa. This can bring mental calm and clarity. The spiritual aspect of yoga entails meditation which is actually the major focus of the practice and not the asanas. Essentially, all yoga practices exist to prepare you to meditate effectively, and to support your meditation practice. When there is stillness and ease in the body (asanas), and evenness in the breath (pranayam), the mind is in a much better position to meditate properly.

Origin & Spread

The origins of yoga have been speculated to be most likely developed around the sixth and fifth centuries BCE in ancient India. Yoga gurus from India later introduced yoga to the west. In the 1980s, yoga became popular as a system of physical exercise across the Western world. The number of American yoga practitioners has increased to over 36 million in 2016.

Motivations for yoga practioners

Unlike stretching or fitness, yoga is more than just physical postures.Even within the physical practice, yoga is unique because we connect the movement of the body and the fluctuations of the mind to the rhythm of our breath. Connecting the mind, body, and breath helps us to direct our attention inward. Through this process of inward attention, one learns to recognize their habitual thought patterns without labeling them, judging them, or trying to change them. They become more aware of our experiences from moment to moment. The awareness that is cultivated is what makes yoga a practice, rather than a task or a goal to be completed. Where do people practice yoga?

Some experts have suggested that having fresh air in a quiet and clean place suits the concentration and awareness that yoga will create. They also suggested not practising yoga in direct sunlight, cold winds or after sun-bathing.

This chart depicts where people in the US are likely to practice Yoga. A large number of people can be seen to practice it at home or at the gym.

When and how often is yoga practiced?

Some experts suggested that the best time for practicing yoga is in the morning before breakfast with the second most conducive time being early evening, around sunset. Others have recommended changing yoga routines according to seasons. One can find the time of the day that best works for them as various people may see different amount of benefits. Yoga should be practiced at least three hours after your latest meal since digestion uses a lot of energy, which will be needed while doing yoga.

Experts also recommended practicing 3 times per week is ideal but a little bit of yoga every day is better than a lot of yoga once or twice per week. The more you practice yoga, the more benefits are experienced. One shouldn't be constrained by time and can practice a bit allowing for natural expansion of time.
